Section 1

How the platform works

Fanometrix Pulse has three core building blocks. Understanding how they relate to each other makes everything else straightforward.

The three building blocks

◫ Survey

The questionnaire. A set of up to 3 questions with multiple-choice answers. A survey is a reusable template — it is not tied to any specific campaign or publisher. The same survey can be used in dozens of campaigns simultaneously.

◎ Campaign

One survey running for one publisher, with specific dates, a response target, and a live/paused/closed status. The campaign controls whether responses are being collected right now. Each campaign gets its own embed code.

⬡ Campaign Group

A bundle of campaigns served through a single embed code. When the embed loads, Fanometrix automatically picks an eligible campaign from the group and serves it. The publisher only needs one URL.

📊 Response

An individual fan's answers. Every response is linked to the exact campaign that served it — not just the group or the survey. This means reporting is always accurate even when groups are rotating between campaigns.

A simple example

Suppose Carlsberg want to run three different surveys on FotMob in June 2026:

  1. You create three Surveys — one for each research topic.
  2. You create three Campaigns — one per survey, all pointing at FotMob, all with a June date range.
  3. You create one Campaign Group called "Carlsberg FotMob June" and add all three campaigns to it.
  4. You send FotMob one embed URL: /embed?group=carlsberg_fotmob_june
  5. Each time a fan loads a page, one of the three surveys is shown. Responses link back to the specific campaign served.
  6. If you want to stop one survey mid-month, you simply pause that campaign in the admin. The other two keep running. FotMob doesn't need to change anything.
Key principle

Surveys are templates. Campaigns are delivery vehicles. Campaign Groups are playlists. Publishers get one URL and you control everything else from the admin.

Section 2

Surveys

A survey is a questionnaire — up to 3 questions, each with 2–6 multiple-choice answers. Surveys are reusable templates that sit independently of campaigns. You build them once and attach them to as many campaigns as you like.

Important

A survey is never Live. Campaigns control whether responses are being collected — not the survey. The same survey can be used in one campaign that is Live, one that is Paused, and one that is Closed, all at the same time.

Creating a survey

1
Go to Survey Templates in the left navigation and click + Create Survey.
2
Give the survey a name (e.g. "Carlsberg England Fans — Brand Awareness") and an optional description.
3
Set the status to Draft while you're writing questions.
4
Add up to 3 questions. Click + Add question for each one, then add 2–6 answer options per question.
5
Customise the Thank You screen — the message fans see after completing the survey.
6
Click Preview to see how the survey looks in the 300×250 MPU format. No responses are recorded in preview mode.
7
When the survey is approved, change the status to Ready and save. It can now be attached to campaigns.

What you can see on each survey card

  • Number of questions
  • Created date and last updated date
  • Used by: N campaigns — click this to see exactly which campaigns are using it
  • Serving in: N live campaigns — how many campaigns using this survey are currently live
  • Last used — when the most recent non-draft campaign using this survey started
  • Last response — how long ago the most recent fan response came in (e.g. "3 hours ago")

Survey Usage modal

Click Used by: N campaigns on any survey card to open a detailed table showing every campaign linked to that survey, including the publisher, status, date range and response count. This is useful when deciding whether it is safe to archive or edit a survey.

Deletion rules

You can only delete a survey if

It has zero responses AND is not linked to any campaigns. If either condition is not met, the Delete button is disabled and you will see an explanation. Archive instead — archived surveys remain searchable and can be restored at any time.

Duplicating a survey

Click Duplicate on any survey card to create an identical copy. The copy is named "Original Survey Name (Copy)", starts as Draft, and has zero counts. This is useful for creating seasonal variations of an existing survey without starting from scratch.

Automatic transitions

Fanometrix automatically moves a campaign from Scheduled → Live when the start date arrives, and from Live → Closed when the end date passes or the target response count is reached. You don't need to do anything manually for these transitions.

Creating a campaign

1
Go to Campaigns and click + Create Campaign.
2
Enter the Brand Name (e.g. "Carlsberg") and Campaign Name (e.g. "UCL 2026 Fan Survey").
3
Set the Campaign ID — this is the slug used in embed URLs (e.g. carlsberg_ucl_2026). Click Auto to generate it from the brand and campaign names. Lowercase and underscores only.
4
Set the Start Date and End Date. If you set both, the campaign goes live automatically on the start date and closes automatically on the end date.
5
Optionally set a Target Responses number. When this is hit, the campaign closes automatically.
6
Select the Survey to attach. Only surveys with status Ready appear in the dropdown. Draft surveys show as "(Draft)" and can still be selected.
7
Add one or more Publishers by typing the publisher name and clicking Add.
8
Leave the status as Draft for now. Click Save Campaign.
9
When you're ready to deploy, use the action buttons on the campaign card to Publish (sets it to Scheduled) or Go Live Now (makes it immediately Live).

Deletion rules

Campaigns cannot be deleted if they have responses

Even one response permanently prevents deletion, to protect reporting integrity. Archive the campaign instead. If you need to delete a campaign that has collected some responses, contact your system administrator.

Tabs on the Campaigns page

  • Active — Draft, Scheduled, Live and Paused campaigns (currently operational)
  • Closed — campaigns that have finished collecting responses
  • Archived — campaigns moved out of the main view for tidiness
  • Deleted — soft-deleted campaigns, visible to admins only; can be restored
Section 4

Campaign Groups

A Campaign Group bundles multiple campaigns together under one embed code. When the embed loads, Fanometrix automatically picks an eligible campaign from the group and serves it to the fan. Each response is still linked to the specific campaign that was served, so reporting remains accurate.

When to use a Campaign Group

  • You want to run multiple surveys on the same publisher placement at the same time
  • You want to rotate between surveys without the publisher updating their embed code
  • You want a single fallback if one campaign pauses or closes mid-flight
Publisher perspective

The publisher gets one URL and trafficks it once. You can then change which surveys run, add new campaigns, pause individual ones, or close the whole group — all without asking the publisher to do anything.

Creating a Campaign Group

1
Go to Campaign Groups in the left navigation and click + Create Group.
2
Enter a Group Name (e.g. "Carlsberg Research Wave 1") and click Auto to generate the slug, or type your own.
3
Enter the Publisher for this group (e.g. FotMob). This is for your reference and appears on the group card.
4
Optionally set Start Date and End Date for the group itself. The group will only serve campaigns within this window.
5
Choose the Rotation Type (see table below).
6
Search for and add the Campaigns you want in this group. Any campaign can be added, but only live campaigns within their date range and below their response target will actually be served at embed time.
7
Save the group with status Draft. When ready to go live, click Go Live on the group card.

Rotation types

TypeHow it worksBest for
EqualEach eligible campaign has the same chance of being selected on every load.Most situations. Default choice.
WeightedCampaigns with higher weight values are served more often. Set weights when editing individual group members.When you want one survey to run more frequently than others.
PriorityThe eligible campaign with the lowest priority number is always served first. Others act as fallbacks if the top-priority campaign is not eligible.When one survey must always serve if possible, with others as backups.

Eligibility — what gets served at embed time

Every time the embed loads, Fanometrix checks each campaign in the group against these rules:

  • The group must be Live and within its date range
  • The campaign must have status Live
  • The campaign must be within its own start and end dates
  • The campaign must not have reached its target response count
  • The campaign's survey must exist and not be deleted

Any campaign that fails these checks is skipped on that load. If no campaigns are eligible, the embed renders blank (the publisher sees an empty ad slot, not an error).

Controlling the group mid-flight

  • To stop one survey temporarily: go to Campaigns and Pause that individual campaign. The group keeps running with the remaining eligible campaigns.
  • To stop everything: set the group status to Paused or Closed. All campaigns in the group stop serving immediately.
  • To swap in a new survey: create a new campaign, add it to the group. It starts serving as soon as it goes Live.
  • To remove a campaign from the group: edit the group and remove it from the campaign list.

The embed code for a group

/embed?group=carlsberg_research_wave1_fotmob&publisher=fotmob&placement=homepage-mpu&country=%%COUNTRY%%

You can copy this directly from the group card with the Copy button. See Section 5 for a full explanation of each parameter.

Section 5

Embed codes

The embed is a standard 300×250 iframe that publishers drop into their ad serving system. Once it's trafficked, you control everything from the Fanometrix admin.

Two types of embed

TypeURL formatWhen to use
Single campaign /embed?campaign=campaign_slug When you want exactly one campaign to serve from this placement
Campaign Group /embed?group=group_slug When you want multiple campaigns to rotate through this placement

URL parameters explained

ParameterRequired?What it does
campaign=Yes (single)The campaign slug. Found on the campaign card in admin.
group=Yes (group)The group slug. Found on the group card in admin.
publisher=RecommendedThe publisher name. Appears in reporting. E.g. fotmob
placement=RecommendedWhere on the publisher's site the embed appears. E.g. homepage-mpu, match-centre-mpu
country=RecommendedThe viewer's country. Use your ad server's macro so it fills in automatically. E.g. %%COUNTRY%% for Google Ad Manager.
survey=OptionalA specific survey UUID. Only used for single-campaign embeds when you need to override the survey.
club=OptionalThe club or team context. Appears in reporting.
competition=OptionalThe competition context (e.g. ucl, premier-league). Appears in reporting.

<iframe src="https://fanometrix-surveys.vercel.app/embed?group=carlsberg_fotmob_june&publisher=fotmob&placement=homepage-mpu&country=%%COUNTRY%%" width="300" height="250" frameborder="0" scrolling="no" style="border:none; display:block;" ></iframe>

You can copy a fully formatted version of this from the Campaign Deployment page in the admin. Select your campaign or group, publisher, placement, and ad server — the code is generated automatically.

Testing before going live

Always test the embed in a browser before sending to the publisher. Open the URL directly (without the country macro) to confirm the survey loads correctly. The Embed Test page in the admin also lets you preview the embed with a preview badge so you can confirm the layout without recording any responses.

Section 6

User Management

Fanometrix accounts are organisation-based. Each account represents a company — a brand, agency, or publisher — rather than an individual. No personal data (names, emails) is stored.

Account roles

RoleWhat they can see
AdminEverything. Full platform access including user management, all campaigns and all surveys.
BrandDashboard, Campaign Reports, Exports, and Insights — but only for the campaigns they are allowed to access.
AgencyDashboard, Campaign Reports, Publisher Performance, and Exports — but only for their allowed campaigns and publishers.
PublisherDashboard and Publisher Performance — filtered to their own publisher data only.

Creating an account

1
Go to User Management and click + New Account.
2
Set a Username — lowercase letters, numbers, underscores and hyphens only. E.g. carlsberg_client
3
Set a Password or click ⚡ Generate Temporary Password to create a strong random password automatically.
4
Leave Force password change on first login ticked. This means the user will be prompted to set their own password the first time they log in — you never need to know their permanent password.
5
Select the Role and enter the Organisation Name.
6
Set Campaign Access if you want to restrict this account to specific campaigns. Leave blank to give access to all campaigns. Search by campaign name to find and select the right ones.
7
Set Publisher Access if you want to restrict this account to data from specific publishers only. Leave blank for all publishers.
8
Click Create Account. A credentials panel appears showing the username and temporary password. Copy these now — the password cannot be viewed again.

Sharing credentials

After creating an account, a Account Ready panel appears with the username and temporary password. Click Copy Credentials to copy both to your clipboard, then share with the client via Teams, Slack, email, or WhatsApp. The password is only shown once.

First login experience

When the client logs in for the first time, they are immediately taken to a Create New Password screen before they can access any data. Once they set their own password, they proceed to the platform. You will never know their permanent password.

Resetting a password

Go to User Management, click Edit on the account, generate or type a new temporary password, tick Force password change on first login, and save. Share the new temporary password with the user. They will be prompted to set a new permanent password on their next login.

Disabling an account

Click Disable on the account row. The account is immediately blocked from logging in. Click Enable to restore access. No data is deleted.

Section 7

Reporting

Every response collected through a Fanometrix embed is stored in the database and linked to the specific campaign that served it. This means you can filter and segment responses by campaign, publisher, placement, country, device, and more.

What is captured on every response

FieldDescription
CampaignWhich campaign served this response
SurveyWhich survey was shown
PublisherWhich publisher's site this came from
PlacementWhich placement on the publisher's site
CountryThe viewer's country (from ad server macro)
DeviceDesktop, mobile or tablet
BrowserChrome, Safari, Firefox etc.
Response timeHow long the fan took to complete the survey
Q1, Q2, Q3The answer chosen for each question
TimestampWhen the response was submitted

Demo data

The Demo Data page lets you generate sample responses for any campaign. These are flagged as demo data and are excluded from all reporting and response counts. They are useful for testing that an embed is set up correctly before real fans respond.

Demo responses can be deleted at any time from the Demo Data page without affecting any real data.

Looker Studio

For advanced reporting, the platform exposes a reporting view (vw_campaign_responses) that connects directly to Looker Studio via the PostgreSQL connector. Contact your administrator for the Looker Studio connection details.

Data integrity

Campaigns and surveys that have collected real responses can never be hard-deleted. They can be archived or soft-deleted, but the underlying response data is always preserved. This protects your reporting and audit trail.

Common questions

Can I use the same survey in two campaigns at once?

Yes. A survey is just a template. You can attach it to as many campaigns as you like, and all of them can be Live simultaneously. The responses will be separated by campaign in reporting.

What happens if all campaigns in a group are paused?

The embed renders blank — publishers see an empty ad slot. No error is shown. As soon as any campaign becomes eligible again (e.g. you resume one), the embed starts serving again automatically.

Can a publisher serving a group embed see which campaign was served?

No. Publishers see only the iframe. The campaign selection happens on the server. The campaign ID is returned to the embed player and used for response submission, but it is not visible to the publisher.

Can I change the survey attached to a live campaign?

Yes, by editing the campaign. However, this will change the questions shown to fans from that point forward. If you have collected responses under the old survey, consider whether changing mid-campaign will affect reporting consistency. It's usually safer to close the old campaign and create a new one.

What does "Archive after N days" mean on a campaign?

After a campaign closes, Fanometrix will automatically move it to Archived status after the number of days you specify (default 90). This keeps the Closed tab tidy without you having to manually archive old campaigns.

How do I give a client access to their campaign data only?

When creating their account, use the Campaign Access selector to choose only the campaigns they should see. Their dashboard and reports will be filtered to those campaigns automatically.
